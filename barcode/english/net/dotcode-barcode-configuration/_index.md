## What is DotCode Barcode Aspect Ratio?
The **dotcode barcode aspect ratio** defines the relationship between the barcode’s width and height. Adjusting this ratio helps you fit the barcode into constrained spaces, maintain visual balance, and improve scanner performance on various surfaces.

## Why Adjust the Aspect Ratio?
- **Design flexibility:** Match the barcode to your UI layout or printed label size.  
- **Scanning reliability:** Certain scanners perform better with wider or taller patterns.  
- **Brand consistency:** Keep the barcode’s appearance aligned with your corporate style guide.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I change the aspect ratio after the barcode image is generated?**  
A: No. The aspect ratio must be set before rendering; you need to regenerate the image after any change.

**Q: Does adjusting the aspect ratio affect data capacity?**  
A: The data capacity remains the same; only the visual dimensions change.

**Q: Which .NET versions are supported by Aspose.BarCode for these features?**  
A: .NET Framework 4.5+, .NET Core 3.1+, .NET 5, .NET 6, and later.

**Q: Are there any performance considerations when using very wide or tall ratios?**  
A: Extremely disproportionate ratios may increase rendering time slightly and could affect scanner readability; test with your target devices.

**Q: How do I obtain a temporary license for testing?**  
A: Register on the Aspose website, download the free trial, and follow the licensing guide included in the package.
